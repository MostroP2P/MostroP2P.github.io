9. Frequently Asked Questions

Is this legal?

Mostro is open-source software for peer-to-peer trading. The legality of operating a P2P exchange depends on your jurisdiction. In most countries, person-to-person Bitcoin trading is legal, but some jurisdictions may require licenses for money transmission or exchange services. We strongly recommend consulting local regulations and legal counsel. The Mostro project does not provide legal advice.

How much does it cost to run?

Monthly costs: $20-30 for a VPS. That's it for infrastructure.

Capital requirement: You'll need some Bitcoin for Lightning liquidity â€” start with 0.01-0.05 BTC (~$500-2,500 at current prices). This isn't spent â€” it's working capital that stays yours.

Time: ~1-3 hours per week for monitoring, disputes, and maintenance.

Do I need to be technical?

You need someone on your team who can follow technical instructions â€” someone comfortable working in a terminal and editing configuration files. They don't need to be a developer. We have step-by-step guides, and we provide hands-on assistance during setup. Think of it like assembling IKEA furniture: you don't need to be a carpenter, but you need to follow the instructions.

What if something goes wrong?

You'll have support from the Mostro development team and a community of other node operators. Common issues have documented solutions. For bugs, you can report them on GitHub and the team responds quickly. Remember: the core technology has been running since 2021.

How much can I earn?

It depends entirely on your trade volume. A community doing $10,000/month in trades generates about $42/month in net fee revenue. At $50,000/month, that's $210. Don't start a Mostro node primarily for the money â€” start it because your community needs it. The revenue will follow as you grow.

What if I don't have enough Lightning liquidity?

Start small. Even 0.01 BTC (~1 million sats) is enough to facilitate small trades. As your community grows and more trades happen, you can add liquidity incrementally. Your users will tell you when they need more â€” trades will start failing for insufficient capacity. Grow with demand.

Can my node be shut down?

Your node runs on a server you control, communicating over Nostr (which has no central server). To shut down your node, someone would need to specifically target your server. You can mitigate this by running behind Tor or a VPN. And even if your node goes down, the rest of the Mostro network keeps running. Your users can switch to other nodes.

What about the risks?

Let's be honest:

  • Mostro is early-stage software. While the core escrow mechanism is battle-tested since 2021, Mostro itself is actively being developed. Bugs are possible. We haven't had any loss-of-funds incidents, but we can't guarantee zero risk.
  • Lightning Network risks exist. Force-closing channels during high-fee periods can be expensive. Channel management requires attention.
  • Capital is required. Your Lightning liquidity is working capital. While it remains yours, it's locked in channels and can't be used for other purposes while deployed.
  • Time investment is real. Running a node well requires consistent attention. Don't underestimate the time commitment for dispute resolution.
  • Volume takes time. Most nodes take months to build meaningful trade volume.

We believe in being transparent about these risks. This is real infrastructure, not a passive income scheme.

Can I change the fees later?

Yes. You can adjust your fee percentage, supported currencies, payment methods, and order limits at any time by editing your configuration file and restarting.

What happens during disputes?

Your designated arbiters (trusted community members) investigate the dispute, communicate with both parties, review evidence, and make a decision. They can either release the Bitcoin to the buyer or cancel the trade (returning Bitcoin to the seller). It's a serious responsibility â€” choose your arbiters carefully.

Do I need to run my own Lightning node?

Yes, Mostro requires an LND (Lightning Network Daemon) node. Options include:

  • Running LND on the same server
  • Using a node-in-a-box solution like Start9 (which has a Mostro package for one-click install)
  • Using a hosted service like Voltage.cloud
  • Connecting to an existing LND node in your community
